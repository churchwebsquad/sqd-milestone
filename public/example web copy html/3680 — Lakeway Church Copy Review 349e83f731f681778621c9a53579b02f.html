    H2: Built to Last. Still Here for the Same Reason.

    Copy: Lakeway Church began in 1972, when this community was still a quiet place, made up largely of retirees and second homes tucked near the lake. A small group of faithful people felt called to plant something in this neighborhood. Not something flashy. Something lasting.

    They wanted a church built on the authority of Scripture, shaped by prayer, and rooted in genuine community. A place where families could feel safe, where people could be known, and where following Jesus was the point of everything.

    That original conviction hasn't changed. Over the decades, Lakeway grew into one of the fastest-growing suburbs outside Austin, and the church grew right alongside it, welcoming new families, adapting to each new season, and expanding ministry to serve every generation. The completion of the new Worship Center in 2021 opened a meaningful new chapter, adding capacity and strengthening what the church does best: bringing people together around Jesus.

    SECTION 5 — WHAT WE BELIEVE

    H2: What We Believe

    Copy: Lakeway Church holds to historic, orthodox Christian faith. These beliefs are the bedrock of everything we teach, preach, and live. We hold them with conviction and share them with clarity, because we believe clarity is kindness.

    [Design Note: Either a group of cards or accordion to display this content]

    About God: God is one. He is the living and true God, everlasting and infinite in power, wisdom, and goodness. He is the creator and sustainer of all things, both visible and invisible.

    About Jesus Christ: Jesus Christ is the Son of God, both fully God and fully man. Born of the Virgin Mary, He suffered and died on the cross for the forgiveness of our sins. He was buried, rose from the dead, and ascended to heaven. He is our Savior, and He will one day return to establish God's eternal kingdom.

    About the Holy Spirit: The Holy Spirit is one in being with the Father and the Son. He convicts the world of sin, righteousness, and judgment, and comforts, sustains, and empowers the faithful, guiding them into all truth.

    About the Bible: The Scriptures are the sole source and criterion for the Christian faith. The inspired Word of God, without error, and the final authority in matters of faith and life. In Scripture we are met by the living Christ and experience His redeeming grace.

    About Human Beings: We are made in the spiritual image of God and are the supreme object of His creation. All people are shaped by an attitude of disobedience toward God, what Scripture calls sin, and are in need of redemption and salvation.

    About Justification: God reaches out to the repentant believer with justifying grace and pardoning love. The shed blood of Jesus Christ and His resurrection provide the only ground for justification and salvation for all who believe. The Holy Spirit bears witness with our spirit that we are children of God.

    About Eternal Security: The true believer is secure in salvation for eternity. Salvation is maintained by the grace and power of God, not by self-efforts or works.

    About Eternity: People were created to exist forever. Eternally separated from God by sin, or eternally with God through forgiveness and salvation. Eternal life with Him is in the Kingdom of God.

    H2: How the Garden Began

    Copy: The vision for the Memorial Garden grew out of a generous community of faith with eyes toward the future. In the 1970s, Phyllis and Donald Oliver first brought the idea for a columbarium on the church grounds to the Trustees. The idea was ahead of its time and was initially set aside.

    In 1995, the Catholic and Protestant congregations went their separate ways, and the church continued caring for the Garden on its own. By 2010, the Memorial Garden property had been deeded separately from the rest of the church property. New guidelines were established and a policy was put in place limiting future purchases to Lakeway Church members and their families.

    Over the years, the Garden expanded with more walls, a prayer area with a waterfall and Praying Jesus, additional landscaping, and other meaningful enhancements.

    SECTION 3 — THE GARDEN TODAY

    H2: The Garden Today

    Copy: The Memorial Garden has grown into a place of remarkable beauty and quiet purpose. Expansion efforts have brought the total to 550 spaces. The prayer area has been doubled in size, creating a peaceful space for meditation, reflection, or small gatherings.

    The Kilgore Memorial, the open Bible, was moved from the back of the Garden to the expansion area, where it now stands as a centerpiece of the extended grounds. Landscaping and fencing have been updated throughout, blending the original Garden with the new, and the entire space is now connected to the Great Lawn through a new entrance.

    The Celtic cross, placed at the dedication in 1990, remains at the heart of the Garden.
